A . P . J Abdul Kalam

1931 - 2015

Missile Man of India

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam, also known as the "Missile Man of India," was a world-renowned scientist, engineer, and statesman who served as the President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was born on October 15, 1931, in the small village of Rameswaram in Tamil Nadu, India, and rose to become one of the most influential figures in the country's history. Dr. Kalam was an accomplished scientist who made significant contributions to the development of India's space and missile programs. He was the chief scientific advisor to the Government of India and the architect of India's successful Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998. In addition to his scientific achievements, Dr. Kalam was also a passionate and dedicated public servant. He was known for his unwavering commitment to improving the lives of the Indian people and for his vision of a stronger, more prosperous India. He was deeply concerned with issues of education, youth development, and science and technology, and he worked tirelessly to promote these causes throughout his career. Dr. Kalam was a humble and unassuming man who inspired millions with his wisdom and leadership. He was a role model and mentor to countless young people and was revered as a true national hero. He was also a gifted writer and speaker, and his books and lectures were widely read and admired. Dr. Kalam's untimely death in 2015 was a great loss to the nation and the world, but his legacy lives on through the countless people he inspired and the countless lives he touched. He will always be remembered as a visionary leader, a brilliant scientist, and a true patriot.
